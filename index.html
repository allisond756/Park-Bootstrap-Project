About the Everglades

The Everglades are a 2 million acre subtropical wetland ecosystem. Located in south and central Florida and is home to a wide variety of wildlife such as the West Indian manatete, American alligator, Atlantic bottlenose dolphin, and the endangered Florida Panther. More than 360 bird species can be found in the Everglades National Park. Including many wading birds like the white and glossy ibies, roseate spoonbills, egrets, and herons. It is also one of the only places in the world where alligators and crocodiles coexist.

How to Visit

Because the Everglades covers such an expansive area there are multiple entrances through out the park. To travel through the park you can walk along the marked trails or purchase an airboat ticket to travel through the many different wetlands in the park.
